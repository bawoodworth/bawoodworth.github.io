1. Intro to Velodyne

Overview of the Velodyne workflow and required software

***Add more details about Velodyne, CGS unit system, dynes as a unit of force etc...

The workflow for creating a Velodyne simulation requires the following steps: CAD, Meshing, Pre-Processing, Computation, and Post-Processing.

For these lessons, we will focus on the last 3 steps: Pre- and Post-Processing, and Computation.

The software required for these steps, is as follows: LS-PrePost, VisIt, Notepad++, and PuTTY. All of this software can be found for free online. LS-PrePost and VisIt are made available by Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory. Notepad++ and PuTTY can be downloaded for free from these links.

Throughout these lessons, you will be provided with meshed models to begin with. Meshing is the bane of many computational analysts, and can be quite time consuming. While it would be useful to develop experience in meshing techniques, here we will focus on setting up simulations and interpreting the results. (i.e. Licenses for meshing software are ridiculous)

LS-PrePost will be used for pre-processing the models and preparing them for simulation. Pre-processing allows us to get all of our ducks in a row, before sending our models off to Velodyne. In LS-PrePost we can organize our nodes into sets, name and manage our parts, and tie up any loose ends leftover from the meshing process.

Post-processing and visualization will be done using VisIt. VisIt is of course short for "visualize it" (I assume). In VisIt, we can parse out the data generated by Velodyne into contours on the model. Some of the types of contours that can be visualized are velocity-magnitude, von mises stress, pressure, strain, etc.

Now that we've all been introduced, go get the software.
